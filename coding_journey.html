I found it very enjoyable, but didn't think much of it until I started to apply for university courses. Thinking about that small amount of coding I had enjoyed in year 10, I decided to give coputer science a go.

I was very nervous about enrolling in computer science as I really had no experience in code and wondered if all my peers would have learnt some at school. So, before the first semester started, I decided to learn a bit of python myself on Sololearn. It turned out that that put me quite ahead of the beginning coursework, and I was able to get on top of all the information. I ended up really loving python and doing very well in it.

Coding at University

I was very nervous about enrolling in computer science as I really had no experience in code and wondered if all my peers would have learnt some at school. So, before the first semester started, I decided to learn a bit of python myself on Sololearn. It turned out that that put me quite ahead of the beginning coursework, and I was able to get on top of all the information. I ended up really loving python and doing very well in it.

The next semester I learnt object oriented programming using java, which I also did well in.

The third language we took on was C in "Systems Programming". I found this to be a very challenging language and course, especially with the changes that covid brought to learning at university. I certainly struggled in this subject at the beginning. I did not pass the first time but as I reviewed and applied myself more to the learning I eventually got a good grasp of C. Even though I at first struggled with it, C is a langauge I really love working in and I hope to continue working with it in the future. It is a challenge but it is great as a programmer to be able to control memory allocation and get a greater view and control of what is happening in memory with each line of code.

Other Computer Science Skills at University
At university I also studied non-language based computer science subjects that I absolutley loved. My favourite were "Models of Computation", "Algorithm Design" and "Algebra and Logic". These sparked both my love of logical problem solving and challenged my creativity. Creating algorithms to solve problems is something I love and discovering different ways at looking at logical patterns and callenges was such a valuable and exciting part of my university education. I would love to continue my learning in these areas.
